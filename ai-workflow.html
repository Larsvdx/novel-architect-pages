AI workflow

AI gets the brief. You still write the book.

Novel Architect does not start with an empty chat box. Each request is built from the book you are working in, the context you select, and the task you choose.

What stays in your hands

The workflow starts with a writer-made plan.

For chapter drafting, Novel Architect sends the skeleton you wrote for the chapter, not a vague instruction to “write the next chapter.” You choose which previous chapters and context entries should travel with the request, then the app turns that bundle into a background AI job.

01 / Chapter drafting

Turn a skeleton into prose.

Draft the chapter skeleton in the editor first: beats, scene purpose, emotional turn, key reveals, or whatever outline style you use. Then open the AI dialog and select the context and earlier chapters that should guide the draft.

You choose

Previous chapters, active context entries, model tier, creativity, and the chapter skeleton.

The app sends

Book details, book AI prompt, selected context, selected previous chapters, and your skeleton.

You get back

A generated story version saved locally beside the chapter.

02 / Revision pass

Ask for another version without overwriting yours.

Revision is for prose that already exists. Use it when a chapter is close but the pacing, tone, dialogue, or clarity needs another attempt. The result becomes a separate version, so comparison stays easy.

You choose

The chapter to revise, supporting context, previous chapters, model tier, and creativity.

The app sends

The saved chapter text being revised plus the supporting material selected for that request.

You get back

A revision saved as a new chapter version, not a silent replacement.

03 / Editorial reviewer

Get a report instead of a rewrite.

The reviewer is deliberately different from drafting. Pick up to five chapters, add optional focus notes, and ask for criticism you can act on. The result is a saved report in the Reviewer tab.

You choose

Eligible chapters, optional context, model tier, creativity, and review focus.

The app sends

The selected chapter text and supporting material needed to write the report.

You get back

A saved editorial review you can revisit while revising the book.

Background jobs

The app waits for the result so you do not have to.

AI jobs run in the background. Novel Architect shows queued and processing states, saves completed output locally first, and only then acknowledges the job. If something interrupts the app, pending jobs can be reconciled when it starts again.

Novel Architect. AI as a tool, not the author.
